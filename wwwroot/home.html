Navigating IT Career Paths To Become A Software Engineer In Training

I started my IT journey back in 2015. My career goal was to be a software engineer. I started working at Time Warner Cable as a customer support professional and basically provided assistance with billing and also troubleshooted their internet and cable services. This job provided me with great troubleshooting skills I needed to work in the field of information technology.

With those new troubleshooting skills, I went online and studied things I would need to know for troubleshooting a PC. Then I finally had the courage to apply to a service desk job and was hired as a service desk analyst for Stefanini. This posistion added more skills to my plate in regards of troubleshooting OS (i.e. Windows 7, Windows 10, OS X 10.11 El Capitan, etc). I also learned alot about active directory (i.e. giving access, removing access, resetting passwords, etc.). While progressing at Stefanini, I began pursuing my associate's degree in computer science (with a speciailization in computer programming) at Macomb Community College full time.

After a grueling interview in 2017, I was selected to to began my new path with Ascension Technologies as a service desk analyst. One thing stuck out to me from this interview was the question, "Where do I see myself in 5 years?" I told the interviewer that I see myself being a software engineer in 5 years.This job provided tuition reiumbursement for a few semesters which was great for my career. After almost 4 years at Ascension Technologies, I felt that this was my time to resign from my current postion and pursue a role that would put in the pathway of becoming a software engineer.

This is when I started applying to a bunch of postions but had no luck until I received a call back from Nexsys Technologies to interview for their application support analyst role. This was a 45 minute panel interview via Microsoft Teams (due to the pandemic) which went great and I was selected for the role. After several months of working in this role, an opportunity with Quicken Loans/Rocket Mortgage presented itself for me to finally get my foot in the door as a software engineer. This was the DevBuild program which, I had to attend the required workshops and then pass an assessment to be considered for the program. Unfortunately, I failed the assessment the first time. I felt defeated. Then I told myself to not worry about it and to try again after doing more self-study. I applied again, took the assessment, and interviewed twice. After all of the hardwork and prayer, currently, my response to "Where do I see myself in 5 years?" to my previous manager at Ascension is coming to pass. Check Me Out
